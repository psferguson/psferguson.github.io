--- layout: page title: Instrumentation --- As an observational astronomer, I am heavily involved in understanding and improving the infrastructure that allows us to learn about the universe. My experience ranges from developing and commissioning instruments and telescopes to calibration and data validation of large survey level catalogs. I started working on instrumentation at Texas A&M with Dr. Jennifer Marshall and Dr. Darren Depoy. There I developed a TCal a mobile spectrophotometric calibration instrument for imaging systems. Subsequently, as another part of my PhD I worked with Dr. Alex Drlica-Wagner on data validation and calibration for the DECam Local Volume Exploration (DELVE) Survey. More recently I have been working on the Vera C. Rubin Observatory helping with in a number of areas including rapid data analysis for system integration, developing "the monster" reference catalog to be used for photometric calibration during commissioning and the first year or so of LSST operations, acting as an on site observer for integration tests in Chile, and helping to develop software to compute data quality metrics automatically as part of the Rubin Science Pipelines.
